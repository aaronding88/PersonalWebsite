Current Project: Thruster

What it is:

Thruster is a 2D sidescrolling bullet-dodge game. The goal is to survive as long as you can, and the game gets progressively more difficult the longer you stay alive. The game generates different "events" that the player has to tackle, so no one playthrough will be exactly the same, which makes each playthrough interesting and robust. It is powered by Unity and C#.

Why spend time on it?

Thruster started off as a practice project, in an effort for me to get more familiar with Unity and C#. However, as I experimented with designs and executions, I slowly began to earnestly enjoy the gameplay. The playstyle was simple to understand, but difficult to master. The mechanics of play is somewhat unique, yet was still familiar.

So, what does it play like?

The game only takes one input, which for now is left mouse click. It then pushes you towards your cursor. Enemy projectiles are slow, so you'll be able to navigate around the screen dodging projectiles and harmful objects. What's more interesting is that the resulting exhaust does some interesting effects, like reflect bullets, disable missiles, and more!

How are things, then?

Right now, the game is far from complete. While the basic gameplay is there, it's not exactly fun yet.
